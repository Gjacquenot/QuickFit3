$$qf_commondoc_header.start$$ $$qf_commondoc_header.end$$ The standard FCS models come in two flavors: Either the diffusion time $$math:\tau_D$$ (i.e. the average retention time of particles in the focus) or the diffusion coefficient $$math:D$$ are the fit parameter.
  1. confocal FCS:
    • diffusion time $$math:\tau_D$$ as fit parameter:
      • 2D diffusion:
        • Multi-Component Normal 2D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components)
        • Multi-Component Normal 2D Diffusion with different relative brightnesses per species (with non-fluorescing/blinking components)
        • Multi-Component Anomalous 2D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing components)
        • Multi-Component Anomalous 2D Diffusion with different relative brightnesses per species (with non-fluorescing components)
        • Multi-Component Normal 2D Diffusion (with stretched non-fluorescing/blinking components)
      • 3D diffusion:
        • Multi-Component Normal 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components)
        • Multi-Component Normal 3D Diffusion with different relative brightnesses per species (with non-fluorescing/blinking components)
        • Multi-Component Normal 3D Diffusion (with stretched non-fluorescing/blinking components)
        • Normal 3D Diffusion with one non-fluorescing component and analytical derivatives
        • Normal 3D Diffusion with one non-fluorescing component and rotation
        • Normal 3D Diffusion with one non-fluorescing component and flow
        • Multi-Component Anomalous 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing components)
        • Multi-Component Anomalous 3D Diffusion with different relative brightnesses per species (with non-fluorescing components)
        • Oscillatory FCS correlation function
      • nD diffusion:
        • Multi-Component Anomalous 2D/3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing components)
    • diffusion coefficient $$math:D$$ as fit parameter:
      • 3D diffusion:
        • Multi-Component Normal 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components) and wxy+gamma to characterize the focus
        • Multi-Component Normal 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components) and wxy+wz to characterize the focus
        • Multi-Component Normal 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components) and wxy+gamma to characterize the focus, including an afterpulsing term
        • Multi-Component Normal 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components) and wxy+wz to characterize the focus, including an afterpulsing term
        • Normal 3D Diffusion with one non-fluorescing component and flow
  2. circle scanning FCS:
    • diffusion coefficient $$math:D$$ as fit parameter:
      • 3D diffusion:
        • Normal 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components) and wxy+gamma to characterize the focus
        • Normal 3D Diffusion (with non-fluorescing/blinking components) and wxy+wz to characterize the focus
  3. simple decays:
    • Exponential Decays
    • Power Law Decays
    • Reaction Dynamics
