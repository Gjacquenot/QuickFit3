This plugin outputs a variety of fit statistics, that allow to evaluate the goodnes of fit (GoF). All of these parameters are calculated for the range of $$math:K $$ datapoints $$math:(\tilde{\tau}_k, \tilde{g}_k, \tilde{\sigma}_k),\ \ k\in [k_0 .. k_1] $$ selected by the user. First we define from these datapoints the residuals $$math:\tilde{E}_k=\tilde{g}_k-g(\tilde{\tau}_k, \vec{p}) $$ and weighted residuals $$math:\tilde{E}_{\text{weighted},k}=\frac{\tilde{g}_k-g(\tilde{\tau}_k, \vec{p})}{\tilde{\sigma}_k^2} $$. Then we can further note down these statistics (the weighted variants use $$math:\tilde{E}_{\text{weighted},k}$$ instead of $$math:\tilde{E}_k$$

  • sum of squared resdiuals (sometimes also called the residual sum of squares, RSS):

    $$bmath:\chi^2 = \sum_{k=k_0}^{k_1}\tilde{E}_k^2 $$

  • residual average:

    $$bmath:\langle E\rangle= \frac{1}{K}\cdot\sum_{k=k_0}^{k_1}\tilde{E}_{k} $$

  • residual standard deviation:

    $$bmath:\sqrt{\langle E^2\rangle}= \sqrt{ \frac{1}{K}\cdot\sum_{k=k_0}^{k_1}\tilde{E}_{k}^2 - \frac{1}{K^2}\cdot\left[\sum_{k=k_0}^{k_1}\tilde{E}_{k}\right]^2 }=\sqrt{ \left\langle\left[\tilde{E}_{k}-\langle E\rangle\right]^2\right\rangle} $$

  • total sum of squares (TSS):

    $$bmath:TSS = \langle g^2\rangle= K\cdot\sum_{k=k_0}^{k_1}\tilde{g}_{k}^2 - \frac{1}{K}\cdot\left[\sum_{k=k_0}^{k_1}\tilde{g}_{k}\right]^2 $$

  • number of fit parameters NP: number of free parameters in the parameter vector $$math:\vec{p}$$ of the model $$math:g(\tilde{\tau}_k, \vec{p}) $$
  • number of datapoints NR = K
  • degrees of freedom $$math:\mbox{DF}= \mbox{NR}-\mbox{NP}-1$$
  • coefficient of determination

    $$bmath:R^2=1-\frac{\mbox{RSS}}{\mbox{TSS}}=1-\frac{\chi^2}{\langle E^2\rangle} $$

Finally also the residual correlation function $$math:\hat{\gamma}(k)$$, i.e. the autocorrelation function of the index-ordered residuals is calculated. This function gives hints on whether there are any non-random structures in the fit values, which might not be visible in the residuals plots:

$$bmath: \hat{\gamma}(k)=\frac{1}{K}\cdot\sum_{i=k_0}^{k_1-k-1}(\tilde{E}_{i}-\langle E\rangle)\cdot(\tilde{E}_{i+k}-\langle E\rangle) $$

The residuals are displayed together with their running average, where the average over the 10 surrounding values is calculated:

$$bmath: \overline{E}^{(10)}(\tilde{\tau}_k)=\frac{1}{11}\sum_{i=k-5}^{k+5}\tilde{E}_i $$
