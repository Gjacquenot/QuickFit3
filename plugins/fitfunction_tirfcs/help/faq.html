$$qf_commondoc_header.start$$ $$qf_commondoc_header.end$$ $$faq_start$$ Which fit functions should I choose for TIR-FCS/FCCS measurements with a camera (i.e. most imaging TIR-FCS/FCCS)? $$faq_answer$$ If you used a camera for data acquisition, the molecular detection efficiency (MDF) of the microscope is often described by a convolution of a rectangular (square) pixel (pixel size $(a)$ in the image plane), which is convolved with a 3-dimensional Gaussian function, which approximates the point-spread function (PSF). These models may then be used (they are all marked by the name addition rect. pixel):
  • 2D lateral diffusion:
    • TIR-FCS Fit Functions: $$list:fitfunc_inplugin:fitfunctions_tirfcs:fcs_tir_:2D$$
    • 2-pixel TIR-FCCS Fit Functions:
      • with flow: $$list:fitfunc_inplugin:fitfunctions_tirfcs:fccs_tir_:Flow$$
    • 2-color TIR-FCCS Fit Functions:
      • normal diffusion: $$list:fitfunc_inplugin:fitfunctions_tirfcs:fccs_tir_:2D diffusion (xy)$$
      • anomalous diffusion: $$list:fitfunc_inplugin:fitfunctions_tirfcs:fccs_tir_:2D anomalous diffusion$$
