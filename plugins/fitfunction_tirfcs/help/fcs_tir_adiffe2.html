TIR-FCCS fit model 2D anomalous diffusion, 1/e²-radii

This function implements a 1-component anomalous diffusion model for data acquired with a camera in TIR illumination mode. The fit function is defined as: $$bmath:g(\tau)=G_\infty+\frac{1}{N/A_{\text{eff}}}\cdot\frac{1}{a^2}\cdot\left[\mbox{erf}\left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{4\Gamma\tau^\alpha+w_{xy}^2}}\right)+\sqrt{4\Gamma\tau^\alpha+w_{xy}^2}\cdot\left[\exp\left(-\frac{a^2}{4\Gamma\tau^\alpha+w_{xy}^2}\right)-1\right]\right]^2$$ $$bmath:A_{\text{eff}}=\frac{a^2}{\left(\mbox{erf}\left(\frac{a}{w_{xy}}\right)+\frac{w_{xy}}{\sqrt{\pi}\cdot a}\cdot\left(e^{-(a/w_{xy})^2}-1\right)\right)^2}$$ The parameters are:

var.Parameter
G∞constant offset
Nparticle number in the observation volume
Γanomalous diffusion coefficient
αanomality parameter
awidth of a (square) pixel in the image plane (i.e. pixel size of camera divided by the magnification)
wxylateral radius of the point-spread function (PSF), 1/e2-radius
$$math:I$$average measured intensity in focus during measurement
$$math:B$$average background intenisty in focus during measurement
For the fractions we have in addition:
ρ1 = 1 - ρ2 - ρ3

$$bmath:\Delta A_{eff}=0$$ $$bmath:\Delta C=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\Delta N}{A_{eff}}\right)^2+\left(\frac{\Delta A_{eff}\cdot N}{A_{eff}^2}\right)^2}$$ For each component the mean squared displacement after a given time $$math:\tau_{MSD}$$ is calculated, as: $$bmath:\mbox{MSD}_i(\tau_{MSD})=6\cdot\Gamma_i\cdot\tau_{MSD}^{\alpha_i}.$$ Also the time, required for a particle to leave the effective area of the pixel is calculated: $$bmath:\tau_i(MSD)=\left(\frac{A_{eff}}{4\cdot\Gamma_i}\right)^{1/\alpha_i}.$$
