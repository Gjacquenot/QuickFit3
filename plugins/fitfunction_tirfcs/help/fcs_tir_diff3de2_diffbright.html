Model Definition

This function implements a 1-component 3D + 2-component 2D normal diffusion model for data acquired with a confocal microscope in TIR illumination mode, where each component may have different relative molecular brightnesses $(q_i)$. It also contains contributions by fluorophore blinking (triplet ...). The fit function is defined as:$$ref:HASSLER2005:Hassler, Kai; Anhut, Tiemo; Rigler, Rudolf; Gösch, Michael; Lasser, Theo (2005): High Count Rates with Total Internal Reflection Fluorescence Correlation Spectroscopy, Biophysical Journal 88(1), p. L01-L03, doi: 10.1529/biophysj.104.053884$$$$ref:RIES2005:Ries, Jonas; Petrov, Eugene P.; Schwille, Petra (2008): Total Internal Reflection Fluorescence Correlation Spectroscopy: Effects of Lateral Diffusion and Surface-Generated Fluorescence, Biophysical Journal 95(1), p. 390-399, doi: 10.1529/biophysj.107.126193$$ $$bmath: G(\tau)=G_\infty+\frac{1}{N\cdot\left((1-\rho_{2D,1}-\rho_{2D,2})+q_{2D,1}\rho_{2D,1}+q_{2D,2}\rho_{2D,2}\right)^2}\cdot X_{back}\cdot\left(\frac{1-\Theta_{non}+\Theta_{non}\mathrm{e}^{-\tau/\tau_{non}}-\Theta_{trip}+\Theta_{trip}\mathrm{e}^{-\tau/\tau_{trip}}}{1-\Theta_{non}-\Theta_{trip}}\right)\cdot\left[(1-\rho_{2D,1}-\rho_{2D,2})\cdot g_{3D}(\tau)+\rho_{2D,1}\cdot q_{2D,1}\cdot g_2(\tau)+\rho_{2D,2}\cdot q_{2D,2}\cdot g_3(\tau)\right]$$ with $$bmath: g_{3D}(\tau)=\frac{V_\text{eff}}{\pi d_{TRIF}^2\cdot\left(w_{xy}^2+4D_{3D}\tau}\right)}\cdot\left[ \sqrt{ \frac{D_{3D}\tau}{\pi}}+\frac{1-2D_{3D}\tau/d_{TRIF}^2}{2/d_{TRIF}}\cdot w\left(\mathrm{i}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{D_{3D}\tau}}{d_{TRIF}^2}\right)\right]$$ $$bmath: g_{2D}(\tau)=\left(1+\frac{4D_{2D}\tau}{w_{xy}^2}\right)^{-1}$$ In these model functions $(d_{TRIF})$ is the 1/e penetration depth of the evanescent field (in z-direction). The light distribution in z is then given by $(I(z)\propto\exp(-z/d_{TIRF}))$. The function $(w(\mathrm{i}\cdot\xi))$ is the real-part of the Faddeeva function: $[ w(\mathrm{i}\cdot\xi)=\exp(\xi^2)\cdot\mbox{erfc}(\xi) ]$ The background correction is performed by factor $$bmath:X_{back}=\frac{(I-B)^2}{I^2}.$$ The effective volume and focus area are defined as: $$bmath:A_{\text{eff}}=\pi\cdot w_{xy}^2$$ $$bmath:V_{\text{eff}}=2\pi\cdot w_{xy}^2\cdot d_{TIRF}$$ From these the concentration is estimated: $$bmath:c=\frac{N}{V_{\text{eff}}}$$ For the fractions we have in addition: $$bmath:\rho_{3D}=1-\rho_{2D,1}-\rho_{2D,2}$$ For each component the mean squared displacement after a given time $$math:\tau_{MSD}$$ is calculated, as: $$bmath:\mbox{MSD}_i(\tau_{MSD})=6\cdot D_i\cdot\tau_{MSD}.$$ Also the time, required for a particle to leave the effective area of the focus is calculated: $$bmath:\tau_i(MSD)=\frac{A_{eff}}{4\cdot D_i}.$$ The following plot shows the difference between a 3D TIR-FCS and a best-fit 2D TIR-FCS function (both: α=1):
