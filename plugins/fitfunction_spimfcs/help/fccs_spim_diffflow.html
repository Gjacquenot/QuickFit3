SPIM-FCCS fit model: 1-component diffusion+flow, 2-pixel crosscorrelation, 1/√e-radii

This function implements a 1-component diffusion+flow model for data acquired with a camera in SPIM illumination mode. It assumes that the time series of two distant pixels were cross-correlated. The model was taken from:

Wohland T, Shi X, Sankaran J, Stelzer EH (2010): Single plane illumination fluorescence correlation spectroscopy (SPIM-FCS) probes inhomogeneous three-dimensional environments, Optics Express 18(10): 10627-10641, doi: 10.1364/OE.18.010627.
$$jankrieger_phdthesis$$
The fit function is defined as: $$bmath:g(\tau)=G_\infty + \frac{(I-B)^2}{I^2}\cdot\frac{1}{4a^2N}\cdot\left(1+\frac{D\tau}{\sigma_z^2}\right)^{-1/2}\cdot g_{xy}(\tau; d_x, v_x)\cdot g_{xy}(\tau; d_y, v_y)$$ $$bmath:\mathrm{with:} g_{xy}(\tau;v,d)=\frac{2\sqrt{D\tau+\sigma_{xy}^2}}{\sqrt{\pi}}\cdot\left(\exp\left[-\frac{(a+d-v\tau)^2}{4(D\tau+\sigma_{xy}^2)}\right]+\exp\left[-\frac{(a-d+v\tau)^2}{4(D\tau+\sigma_{xy}^2)}\right] -2\cdot\exp\left[-\frac{(d-v\tau)^2}{4(D\tau+\sigma_{xy}^2)}\right] \right)+(a+d+v\tau)\cdot\text{erf}\left(\frac{a+d-v\tau}{2\sqrt{D\tau+\sigma_{xy}^2}}\right) +(a-d+v\tau)\cdot\text{erf}\left(\frac{a-d+v\tau}{2\sqrt{D\tau+\sigma_{xy}^2}}\right)-2(d-v\tau)\cdot\text{erf}\left(\frac{d-v\tau}{2\sqrt{D\tau+\sigma_{xy}^2}}\right)$$ The parameters are:

$$math:v_y$$ flow velocity in y-direction
awidth of a (square) pixel in the image plane (i.e. pixel size of camera divided by the magnification)

$$math:d_y$$ distance between pixels in y-direction

$$math:B$$average background intenisty in focus during measurement
