$$qf_commondoc_header.start$$ $$qf_commondoc_header.end$$ $$funcref_start$$ polyfit(X, Y, n) - fits a polynomial of degree n to the data vectors X and Y : $$funcref_description$$ The argument X and Y have to be vectors with at least n+1 entries. The function returns the n+1 coefficients of the polynomial expression $$bmath:f(x)=\sum_{j=0}^n a_j\cdot x^j$$ as a vector with the entires [a0, a1, ..., an].

Algorithm Description:
This method obtains the least-squares parameters of the polynomial, by solving the linear equation system (using a simple Gauss-Jordan solver): $$bmath:\begin{bmatrix} y_1\\ y_2\\ y_3 \\ \vdots \\ y_n \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & x_1 & x_1^2 & \dots & x_1^P \\ 1 & x_2 & x_2^2 & \dots & x_2^P\\ 1 & x_3 & x_3^2 & \dots & x_3^P \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots \\ 1 & x_n & x_n^2 & \dots & x_n^P \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} p_0\\ p_1\\ p_2\\ \vdots \\ p_P \end{bmatrix}$$ $$bmath: \vec{y}=V\vec{p}\ \ \ \ \ \Rightarrow\ \ \ \ \ \vec{p}=(V^TV)^{-1}V^T\vec{y} $$ The method is e.g. described in https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynomial_regression. $$funcref_end$$ $$see:See also the polynomial functions reference.$$
